My journey learning languages

++ my method and links

I love learning languages. Ever since I was introduced to french as a child in school, and shown the wonders of other cultures. For me learning a new language isn't just a way to communicate with people - it's a way to gain a new perspective on the world, see new ways of thinking, and experience the same thing through a new lense. It's also really fun to be able to surprise someone when I can speak their language and of course "If you talk to a man in a language he understands, that goes to his head. If you talk to him in his language, that goes to his heart."

After learning french in school, and then moving onto spaish as well, I began to look at (~dabble in) many other langauges such as Japanese and Norwegian. After countless weeks of tireless research (youtube) I found my own method to not only continue with Spanish and French but to add Italian and then Portugese to my growing collection of (sort of) spoken romance languages. I decided to branch out to German (/Austrian) next for an upcoming trip. I learnt how to order a beer and after that maybe dropped off a little (but what more do you need let's be honest) but have since vowed to get back on the language train once and for all! I will gain greater understanding in all of these languages before I move on(maybe)!

Just for fun, here's a picture of me in the Pyrenees about 2 weeks after finishing my exams - it was the first time I had truly been able to speak a language with native speakers in the country. I loved it and it gave me so much more determination and motoivation to keep learning.
